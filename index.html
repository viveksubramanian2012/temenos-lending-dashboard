Luxembourg-Specific Gaps — Deposits TBC
Material Gaps for CLIENT-LU-01 — Verify Before Architecture Decision
CAMT.053 Statements Not in Deposits TBC
XML/CAMT/SWIFT statement formats not natively supported by Deposits TBC. For Luxembourg corporate clients relying on CAMT.053 (bank-to-customer account statement), this is material. Must be addressed via an external delivery/statement system — adds integration scope, cost, and a new vendor dependency.
Dormancy → Caisse de Consignation Workflow
Luxembourg's dormancy law (loi du 27 juillet 2012 relative aux comptes inactifs) requires banks to transfer dormant account balances to the Caisse de Consignation after 10 years. Deposits TBC supports dormancy configuration, but the LU-specific workflow — CSSF notification, automated transfer event, and audit evidence — must be confirmed against TBC's dormancy event model before programme scoping.
Facility Property Class Not Available in Deposits TBC
The AA Facility property class (cheques, direct debit, sweeping service flags) is not applicable in Deposits TBC. Activity Restriction is used instead. Banks relying on Facility-driven service blocking must redesign this mechanism using alternative TBC configurations — requires re-analysis and likely functional redesign in the programme.
CSSF 22/806 — Material Outsourcing (If TBC on Temenos SaaS)
Deploying Lending or Deposits TBC on Temenos SaaS constitutes material outsourcing under CSSF 22/806. This requires: prior CSSF notification before go-live, a documented and tested exit strategy, sub-outsourcing risk assessment of Temenos's own cloud provider chain, and annual risk review. Must be incorporated into programme scope and the client's CSSF relationship managed proactively.
